Math

Four domains. No calculator excuses.

Every question is multiple choice or a typed-in answer — no guessing the format

A graphing calculator (built into Bluebook, or a physical one) is allowed on the entire Math section. The skill that matters most isn’t arithmetic speed — it’s recognizing which method a problem is asking for.

Domain 1

Algebra

Linear equations, linear functions, systems of equations, linear inequalities.

 Domain 2

Advanced Math

Nonlinear functions and equations, systems with a nonlinear piece, equivalent expressions.

 Domain 3

Problem-Solving & Data Analysis

Ratios & percentages, one- and two-variable data, probability, statistical inference.

 Domain 4

Geometry and Trigonometry

Area & volume, lines/angles/triangles, right-triangle trig, circles.
